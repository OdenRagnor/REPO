VPNs and Proxy Servers.

Only use VPNs from reputable companies with transparent privacy policies and official download pages. Avoid fully free VPNs with no paid tier, as many rely on data collection or advertising. Free proxies are unsafe and should not be used for privacy or investigation work. Free proxies usually log your web traffic, inject ads, sell your browsing data, are misconfigured or malicious, and are often used as honeypots.

VPNs provide encrypted, system‑wide protection and are generally safer for personal privacy. Proxies do not encrypt traffic and are not designed for anonymity, but they are useful for OSINT tasks that require rotating IPs or large‑scale data collection. Each tool serves a different purpose.

Now that we have made a safe OSINT environment let's dive into more specific trade craft. OSINT is meant to be a low effort way of collecting information on a person, place, or thing.

Let's begin with OSINT Methodology.

Let's begin this section by identifying the five steps of the OSINT Cycle. For the purpose of this class we will call it the OSINT Star as it has five main points.

OSINT Cycle (OSINT Star)



Preparations

This is the step where company or command leadership and analyst identify information gaps. This is where we determine requirements and plan the collection effort, break these requirements into smaller tasks, OSINT planners decide what to collect, how, and with what resources, legal, licensing, operation security (OPSEC), and attribution considerations must be addressed at this level and before collection begins.

Collection

Collection is the acquisition of publicly available information (PAI) in response to the requirements. OSINT collection, collects data and information and exploits it for further usage. collection is shaped by time, topic, scope, human resources, and tools. OSINT may use systematic (routine) and ad-hoc (urgent) collection. Analysts must consider bias, disinformation, OPSEC, legal considerations, and managed attributions. Collection includes the following: selecting sources (media, social media, grey literature, broadcasts, etc.), using overt or discreet methods, Managing subscriptions and commercial sources, Avoiding digital footprints that reveal the collector's interests, and deconflicting with other collection assets.

Processing

Processing converts raw open-source data into usable intelligence through structured steps. Processing includes collation, evaluation, analysis, integration, and interpretation. Collation involved the following: grouping, recording, and filing incoming information, adding metadata, preventing circular reporting. Evaluation includes the following: assessing source reliability and information credibility separately and identifying bias, deception, agendas, and limitations. Analysis includes reviewing information to identify significant facts. Integration includes combining analyzed information with other intelligence to form patterns. Interpretation includes the following: judging the significance of information related to existing knowledge and asking what it means, what it changes, and what it impacts.

Analysis

Analysis identifies significant facts for subsequent interpretation. Integration and interpretation refine said facts into judgments. Analysts must consider deception, bias, and alternative explanations. Analysis includes the following: identifying patterns, anomalies, and trends, challenging assumptions, and validating information with other disciplines.

Dissemination

Dissemination is the process of providing information to users in the format they need and by the time requested. Dissemination includes feedback, which closes the cycle and uses both push (alerts, reports) and pull (databases and request for information (RFI) systems) models. Dissemination includes the following: delivering OSINT products to your clients, ensuring clarity, relevance, and timeliness, archiving products properly, and receiving feedback to refine future collection.

The above topics are discussed more fully in the NATO ALLIED JOINT DOCTRINE FOR OPEN-SOURCE INTELLIGENCE.

You will need to use the least intrusive methods first and only move to more intrusive methods only if the least intrusive methods have failed.

Depending on the organization you will be conducting OSINT analysis for, you will have different guidelines for what is authorized and what is not based off of mission requirements or collection requests. Always refer to your organization's guidelines before moving past basic level PAI collection. Never conduct non-basic OSINT collection for an organization without proper training and mission authority.
